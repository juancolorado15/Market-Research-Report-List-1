<p><p>Livestock anti-infective medicine can be categorized into two types: external use and internal use. External use medicines are applied directly to the animal's skin or coat for treating skin infections, wounds, and parasites. Internal use medicines are administered orally or through injection to treat internal infections and diseases, such as respiratory infections and gastrointestinal disorders. Both types of medicines play a critical role in maintaining the health and well-being of livestock animals by preventing and treating various infections.</p></p>
