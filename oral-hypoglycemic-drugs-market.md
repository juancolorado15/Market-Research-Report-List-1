<p><p>The Oral Hypoglycemic Drugs market comprises several types of medications used to lower blood sugar levels in patients with diabetes. Sulfonylureas (SU) are medications that stimulate the pancreas to produce more insulin, helping to decrease blood sugar levels. Double Guanidine drugs work by increasing the release of insulin and reducing the production of glucose in the liver. Glucosidase Inhibitor medications slow down the digestion and absorption of carbohydrates, thereby preventing spikes in blood sugar levels. Insulin Synergist drugs enhance the effects of insulin in the body, improving glucose uptake and utilization by cells.</p></p>
