<p><p>Casein Hydrolyzed Peptides are commonly categorized into two types based on the method of hydrolysis: Acid Hydrolyzed Casein and Enzymatic Hydrolyzed Casein. Acid hydrolyzed casein is produced by using acid to break down casein proteins into smaller peptides, while enzymatic hydrolyzed casein is made by using enzymes to accomplish the same process. Both types of casein hydrolyzed peptides are used in various industries such as food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics due to their bioactive properties and easy digestibility.</p></p>
