<p><ul><li>Additional Silicone (A-Silicones)</li><li>Condensation Silicone(C-Silicones)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Silicone impression materials are commonly used in dentistry to take accurate impressions of teeth and oral tissues. There are two types of silicone impression materials: Additional Silicones (A-Silicones) and Condensation Silicones (C-Silicones). A-Silicones offer excellent accuracy, dimensional stability, and tear resistance. They are used for crown and bridge impressions. C-Silicones have good dimensional stability but may shrink slightly during setting. They are commonly used for preliminary impressions and denture impressions. Both types of silicone impression materials are crucial in providing accurate and detailed dental impressions for effective treatment planning and fabrication of dental restorations.</p></p>
