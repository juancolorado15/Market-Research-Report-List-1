<p><p>Automotive Whiplash Protection System (WPS) refers to safety features designed to reduce the risk and severity of whiplash injury during rear-end collisions. The market for WPS includes two main types: backrests and head restraints. Backrests are integrated into car seats, employing special structures and materials to absorb and distribute impact forces, reducing the strain on the neck and spine. Head restraints, on the other hand, are adjustable components located at the top of the seat, aimed at limiting excessive head movement and maintaining better alignment with the torso during a crash.</p></p>
