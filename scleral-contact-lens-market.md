<p><p>Scleral contact lenses are a type of contact lens that cover the entire cornea and extend onto the white part of the eye, known as the sclera. These lenses are larger in size than traditional contact lenses and rest on the sclera, creating a fluid-filled chamber between the lens and the cornea. This fluid acts as a cushion and helps to protect and moisturize the cornea, making scleral lenses suitable for individuals with a variety of eye conditions.</p><p>The market for scleral contact lenses has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, advancements in technology have led to the development of more comfortable and breathable scleral lenses, which has increased their acceptance among users. <p><p>The Scleral Contact Lens market is divided into two main types: Rigid Scleral Contact Lens and Soft Scleral Contact Lens.
