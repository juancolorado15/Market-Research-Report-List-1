<p><p>EVA packaging lining is commonly produced through two main methods: injection moulding and compression moulding. Injection moulding involves injecting molten EVA material into a mold to create the desired shape, while compression moulding involves placing solid EVA material into a heated mold where it is compressed into the desired form. Each method has its own advantages and applications, with injection moulding being more suitable for complex shapes and high-volume production, while compression moulding is often used for simpler shapes and lower volume production.</p></p>
