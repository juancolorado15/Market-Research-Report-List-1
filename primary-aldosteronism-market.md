<p><p>Primary Aldosteronism, also known as Conn's syndrome, is a hormonal disorder that occurs when the adrenal glands produce excessive amounts of the hormone aldosterone. Aldosterone plays a crucial role in regulating blood pressure and maintaining the balance of sodium and potassium in the body. However, in Primary Aldosteronism, there is an overproduction of aldosterone, leading to high blood pressure and lower potassium levels.</p><p>The primary cause of this condition is the presence of aldosterone-producing adrenal tumors or adrenal hyperplasia. Common symptoms of Primary Aldosteronism include hypertension, fatigue, muscle weakness, frequent urination, and excessive thirst. If left untreated, it can lead to complications such as heart problems, stroke, and kidney damage.</p><p>The Primary Aldosteronism Market is expected to experience significant growth over the forecast period.
