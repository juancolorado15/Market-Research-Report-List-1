<p><p>Oil absorbent agents are divided into three main types: Natural Organic, Natural Inorganic, and Synthetics. Natural Organic absorbents, such as peat moss or sawdust, are derived from living organisms and have a high absorption capacity. Natural Inorganic absorbents, like clay or sand, are mineral-based materials that effectively soak up oil spills. Synthetics, including polypropylene and polyurethane, are man-made materials designed specifically for absorbing oil. Each type of absorbent offers unique properties and benefits for different oil spill cleanup situations.</p></p>
