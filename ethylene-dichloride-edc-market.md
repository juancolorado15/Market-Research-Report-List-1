<p><p>Ethylene Dichloride (EDC) is produced through two main processes: Direct Chlorination and Oxychlorination. Direct Chlorination involves combining ethylene and chlorine gas to produce EDC, while Oxychlorination involves the reaction of ethylene, oxygen, and hydrogen chloride. The Direct Chlorination method is considered the traditional method and has been widely used historically. Oxychlorination, on the other hand, is a newer and more environmentally friendly process that is gaining popularity in the market. Both methods have their advantages and are used in different industries depending on their specific requirements.</p></p>
