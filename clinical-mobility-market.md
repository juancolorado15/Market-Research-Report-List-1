<p><p>Clinical mobility refers to the use of mobile devices, applications, and solutions in healthcare settings to enhance patient care, improve communication, and increase productivity. Firstly, the increasing adoption of electronic health records (EHRs) and other healthcare IT systems has led to a higher demand for mobile solutions that can access and update patient information remotely. Secondly, the rising prevalence of chronic diseases and the need for telemedicine and remote patient monitoring are driving the demand for clinical mobility solutions. Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has further accelerated the adoption of clinical mobility technologies to support virtual consultations and remote patient care.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in wireless communication technology, such as 5G, have facilitated faster and more reliable connectivity, enabling real-time data exchange and telemedicine applications.
