<p><p>ECT scanners are used in nuclear medicine imaging, with different types catering to specific imaging needs. Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) scanners utilize radioactive tracers to create 3D images of the distribution of a radiopharmaceutical in the body. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) scanners detect gamma rays emitted by a positron-emitting radionuclide to create detailed images of molecular functions in the body. Dual-Head SPECT (D-SPECT) scanners offer improved sensitivity and image quality by utilizing two detector heads to capture images simultaneously from different angles.</p></p>
