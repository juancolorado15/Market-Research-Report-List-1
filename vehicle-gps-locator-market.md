<p><p>Vehicle GPS locators come in three main types: Cellular, Wireless Passive, and Satellite Real Time. Cellular locators use the cellular network to transmit location data, making them suitable for real-time tracking. Wireless Passive locators store data locally and require manual retrieval, making them ideal for periodic monitoring. Satellite Real Time locators use satellite signals for tracking in real time, making them suitable for remote and off-grid locations where cellular signals may be unreliable. Each type offers unique features tailored to different tracking needs.</p></p>
