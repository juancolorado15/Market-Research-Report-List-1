<p><p>The Osteoarthritis Pain Drugs market is categorized into three types: oral, injection, and external. Oral drugs are taken by mouth and include medications like acetaminophen and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). Injection drugs are directly injected into the affected joint to provide localized pain relief, such as corticosteroids or hyaluronic acid. External drugs are applied topically to the skin and include creams, gels, or patches containing NSAIDs or capsaicin. These different types of drugs offer various methods of administration and target different levels of pain associated with osteoarthritis.</p></p>
