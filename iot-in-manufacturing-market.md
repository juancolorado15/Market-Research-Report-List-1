<p><p>IoT in Manufacturing, also known as Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT), is the application of connected devices and intelligent systems to enhance and automate manufacturing processes. The increasing adoption of automation and digitization in the manufacturing sector is a major driving factor for this growth. Manufacturer's growing focus on cost reduction, process optimization, and quality improvement is leading to the widespread implementation of IoT technologies in their operations. Additionally, the need for predictive maintenance, remote monitoring, and real-time analytics is also fueling the demand for IoT in manufacturing.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing use of cloud computing platforms and big data analytics in conjunction with IoT is enabling manufacturers to gain valuable insights from vast amounts of data generated by connected devices. The implementation of edge computing, where data processing takes place closer to the source, is gaining traction as it reduces latency, enhances security, and minimizes bandwidth usage. Additionally, the deployment of 5G networks is expected to revolutionize the IoT in manufacturing by providing faster and more reliable connectivity for a large number of connected devices.</p><p>In conclusion, the IoT in Manufacturing Market is poised for significant growth with a forecasted CAGR of 12.9% during the forecast period.
